
Lab 8: PID Controller Characteristics

Objective: Demonstrate the individual behavior of the Proportional, Integral, and Derivative control modes in open- and closed-loop operation.

What's Covered


Proportional mode: gain vs. proportional band, controller output saturation, and residual steady-state offset
Integral mode: ramping controller output in response to error, and the cause of reset windup
Derivative mode: response to rate-of-change of measurement, and how derivative action gives the controller a time "lead"


Key Takeaway

Each PID term serves a distinct purpose — P reacts to error size, I removes steady-state offset, and D reacts to the rate of change — and understanding them individually is essential before tuning a combined PID controller.
